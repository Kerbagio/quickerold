# QuickER Voice & Tone Guide

## Principles

- **Clear, concise, calm**: Emergency situations require clarity and reassurance
- **No medical advice**: We provide routing help, not diagnosis or medical recommendations
- **Inclusive and accessible**: Support for multiple languages and accessibility needs
- **Professional yet empathetic**: Maintain medical professionalism while showing care

## Key Messaging

### Primary Value Proposition
"Get to the right hospital, faster. Because in emergencies, minutes matter."

### Core Messages
- We provide routing assistance, not medical advice
- Always call 911 for life-threatening emergencies
- Real-time data for informed decisions
- Trusted by healthcare professionals

## Microcopy Examples

### Buttons
- "Find Emergency Care" (not "Search" or "Locate")
- "Check Wait Times" (not "View Times")
- "Emergency" (primary CTA)
- "Get Directions" (not "Navigate")

### Error Messages
- "Unable to find your location. Please enable location services and try again."
- "No emergency facilities found nearby. Consider calling 911 for immediate assistance."
- "Service temporarily unavailable. Please call 911 for emergencies."

### Permission Dialogs
- "QuickER needs access to your location to find nearby emergency care facilities."
- "Allow notifications to receive real-time updates about your route and facility status."

### Safety Reminders
- "Important: Always call 911 for life-threatening emergencies."
- "This tool provides routing assistance only and is not a substitute for professional medical advice."
- "In case of chest pain, difficulty breathing, or severe injury, call 911 immediately."

## Tone Guidelines

### DO Use
- "nearest emergency facility" (instead of "closest hospital")
- "care facility" (instead of just "hospital")
- "estimated arrival time" (instead of "ETA")
- "specialized services" (instead of "specialties")

### DON'T Use
- Medical terminology that patients might not understand
- Urgent language that could increase panic
- Promises about medical outcomes
- Diagnostic language or medical advice

## Accessibility Notes

- Use clear, simple language (8th grade reading level)
- Provide alternative text for all images and icons
- Ensure high contrast for readability
- Support screen readers with proper heading structure
- Include keyboard navigation support
